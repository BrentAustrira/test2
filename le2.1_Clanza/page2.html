Places to see in Switzerland

For such a small country, Switzerland isn't lacking places to go and things to see. Whether it's culture, sightseeing or winter sports, a Switzerland travel adventure will keep you busy. Here's our rundown of the best things to do in Switzerland.

Experience The Aletsch Glacier

This UNESCO World Heritage site really has to be seen to be believed. They say the Aletsch glacier is the longest and the largest in Europe, but no stat can prepare you for this vast sea of ice and bottomless crevasses. Take a ride to the top of either the Bettmerhorn or Eggishorn cable cars to get the best views, or get your mountaineering game on by hiking up the glacier itself. Whichever way you see it, prepare to experience one of the best things to see in Switzerland.

See Lucerne and conquer Mt. Pilatus

The cobbled streets and quaint buildings of this pretty lakeside city make it perfect for a walking mission. Wander its twisting lanes, taking in the famous 14th century Kapellbrücke bridge and the twin-spired Church of St. Leodegar, or take a scenic boat ride on the lake. Fancy something more adventurous? The cog railway to the summit of Mount Pilatus is the steepest of its kind in the world, where the views are nothing short of spectacular. If you're planning to travel to Switzerland then make sure this is on the shortlist.

See Château de Chillon

The beauty of Chillon Castle is unrivalled by any other castle in Europe, and its setting, on the idyllic shores of Lake Geneva at the foot of the towering snowcapped Alps, is every bit as impressive. The view as you stroll along the winding lakeside path from Montreux is one of the country’s most iconic - small wonder it ranks as Switzerland's most visited historic monument.

Rietberg Museum in Zürich

Zürich's third largest museum, the Rietberg is the only museum in Switzerland dedicated to non-European art. Set in the beautiful Rieterpark in central Zürich, it offers a fascinating collection of art from Asia, Africa and Ancient America.

Musée Ariana in Geneva

Another of the essential things to see in Geneva, this museum of ceramics and glass arts is worth the visit for its stunning architecture alone. Its collection of over 20,000 exhibits from the past twelve centuries will make you glad you took the time to visit, though it's worth noting that written descriptions of exhibits are in French only.
